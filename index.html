Depression

Level 1: Causes and Signs

There are multiple types of depression, each with their own range of severity:

  • Persistent Depressive Disorder (formerly known as dysthymia)
  • Major Depression (also known as clinical depression)
  • Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (unique to anyone with a typical set of female reproductive organs)
  • Bipolar Disorder (the really low end meets the criteria for Major Depression)

Each type of depression has their own diagnosis, but common symptoms of these types of depression include:
  • Sleep: too little or too much, restless sleep
  • Lack of concentration, suicidal thoughts, fatigue
  • Behaviorally: Excessive crying, irritability, agitation, social isolation
  • Changes in appetite, such as loss of appetite
  • Rumination (repeatedly going over negative thoughts of one's distress)
